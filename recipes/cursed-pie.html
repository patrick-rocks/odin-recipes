Cursed Pie

This thing is cursed!

A layer of ground beef and vegetables topped with fluffy mash and cursed to perfection.

Equipment: Large saucepan, large pot, potato masher/ricer(preferred), colander, 8x12 baking dish, evil intent

Serves 6-8

Ingredients

  • 1lb ground beef (sub ground lamb for a Shepherd's Pie)
  • 4 large carrots, diced
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 1tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 sprigs rosemary & 3 sprigs thyme, de-stemmed & finely chopped
  • 16oz chicken stock
  • 1tbsp worcestershire sauce
  • 8oz frozen peas
  • 2.5lb Yukon Gold Potatoes, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 8oz heavy cream
  • 1/2 stick butter
  • 2-4oz of cheddar and/or parmesan cheese, grated
  • Salt & pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Start boiling a large pot of water for the potatoes
  2. Heat saucepan on medium high, add the beef, season with salt and pepper, breaking it down as it cooks with a sturdy spatula or spoon
  3. Once the meat is browned, remove from heat and place in a bowl, leaving behind the liquid fat
  4. Add carrots, onion, and a pinch of salt to the pan and cook on medium until the onion softens
  5. Add tomato paste and herbs and cook for 2 minutes, stirring constantly
  6. Add chicken stock, worcestershire sauce, and peas. Bring the mixture to a boil and then simmer on low for 20 minutes
  7. Preheat the oven to 350F
  8. Boil the potatoes until they can be pierced by a fork with little effort
  9. Drain potatoes in the colander then return them to the pot and mash
  10. Add cream, butter, cheese, and a few big pinches of salt, whisk or stir the mash over the lowest heat until completely incorporated and smooth.
  11. Removed from heat. Add more cream, butter, cheese, and/or salt to taste. Keep in mind cream will loosen the mash while cheese will tighten it up.
  12. Once the liquid in the beef mixture has significantly reduced, spoon it into the baking dish and then level it out
  13. Carefully spoon and level the mash on top of the beef without mixing the two
  14. Bake 20-30 minutes or until beef mixture bubbles up along the edges of the dish
  15. Finish under the broiler under careful supervision. Let cool for at least 20 minutes, carve a face with utensil of your choice through the top layer of mash and serve.
